# Company Policies

## Work From Home Policy

### Eligibility
All full-time employees who have completed their probation period (90 days) are eligible for our hybrid work arrangement. Remote work is available up to 3 days per week, with a minimum of 2 days required in the office for collaboration and team meetings.

### Equipment and Setup
- The company provides a laptop, monitor, and necessary peripherals for home office setup
- Employees are responsible for maintaining a dedicated workspace with reliable internet (minimum 50 Mbps)
- IT support is available remotely from 9 AM to 6 PM on business days
- Any equipment issues should be reported within 24 hours

### Working Hours
- Core hours are 10 AM to 4 PM in your local timezone
- Employees must be available and responsive during core hours
- Flexible hours outside core time are permitted with manager approval
- All meetings should be scheduled during core hours when possible

### Communication Expectations
- Daily standup attendance is mandatory (virtual or in-person)
- Response time to Slack messages during working hours: within 2 hours
- Email responses expected within 24 hours
- Calendar must be kept up-to-date with availability status

## Leave Policy

### Annual Leave
- 20 days of paid annual leave per year
- Accrued at 1.67 days per month
- Can be carried forward up to 5 days to the next year
- Must be requested at least 2 weeks in advance for leaves longer than 3 days
- Manager approval required

### Sick Leave
- 12 days of paid sick leave per year
- No medical certificate required for leaves up to 2 consecutive days
- Medical certificate mandatory for 3+ consecutive days
- Unused sick leave does not carry forward

### Public Holidays
- All national and state public holidays are observed
- If a public holiday falls on a weekend, the following Monday is a compensatory off
- List of holidays published at the beginning of each year

### Parental Leave
- Primary caregiver: 26 weeks paid leave
- Secondary caregiver: 4 weeks paid leave
- Must notify HR at least 8 weeks before expected due date
- Flexible return-to-work options available

### Emergency Leave
- Up to 3 days per year for family emergencies
- Manager notification required as soon as possible
- Documentation may be requested

## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and professionalism
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive company and client information
- Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any potential conflicts
- Refrain from harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Represent the company positively in all professional interactions

### Workplace Harassment
We maintain a zero-tolerance policy for:
- Sexual harassment or unwelcome advances
- Discrimination based on race, gender, religion, age, disability, or sexual orientation
- Bullying, intimidation, or threatening behavior
- Retaliation against those who report violations

Any violations should be reported immediately to HR or through our anonymous reporting system.

### Dress Code
- Business casual attire for office days
- Professional appearance required for client meetings (virtual or in-person)
- Casual attire acceptable on Fridays and when working from home
- Use good judgment based on your schedule and meetings

### Social Media Guidelines
- Do not share confidential company information on social media
- Clearly indicate personal opinions are your own, not the company's
- Be respectful when discussing work-related topics
- Do not engage in online harassment or unprofessional behavior

## Security Guidelines

### Data Protection
- All company data must be stored on company-approved platforms
- Use encryption for sensitive data transmission
- Do not store company data on personal devices or cloud services
- Regular backups are mandatory for critical data

### Access Control
- Use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters, mixed case, numbers, symbols)
- Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all company accounts
- Do not share passwords or access credentials
- Lock your computer when away from your desk
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately

### Email and Communication Security
- Verify sender identity before clicking links or downloading attachments
- Be cautious of phishing attempts and suspicious emails
- Do not send sensitive information via unencrypted email
- Use company-approved communication tools only

### Physical Security
- Wear your ID badge at all times in the office
- Do not allow tailgating through secure doors
- Secure confidential documents when not in use
- Shred sensitive documents before disposal
- Report suspicious individuals or activities to security

### Incident Reporting
- Report any security incidents or breaches immediately to IT Security
- Do not attempt to investigate or resolve security incidents on your own
- Preserve evidence and avoid making changes to affected systems
- Cooperate fully with security investigations

### Acceptable Use
- Company resources are for business purposes
- Limited personal use is acceptable during breaks
- Do not access inappropriate, illegal, or offensive content
- Do not use company resources for personal business ventures
- Bandwidth-intensive activities (streaming, gaming) are prohibited

---

## Policy Violations

Violations of company policies may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training or counseling
- Suspension without pay
- Termination of employment
- Legal action in severe cases

The severity of consequences depends on the nature and frequency of violations. All disciplinary actions are documented and kept in employee records.

---

## Policy Updates

These policies are reviewed annually and may be updated as needed. Employees will be notified of any changes via email and company-wide announcements. It is each employee's responsibility to stay informed about current policies.
